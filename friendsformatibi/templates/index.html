OUR APPROACH

We are the only inch wide community development charity that is offering mile deep holistic support through a self-learning innovation center so that people can create their own independent and self-sustaining life, tackle together the most pressing problems of the community and make the community thrive.

WHO WE ARE

We are a Zimbabwean trust responsible for strategy and operation and a Swiss donor association supporting the community in Ward 3 Mwenezi, Matibi, Zimbabwe. We are focussing on holistic support for the community at Matibi through a catalyzing self-learning and self-help center called Nook.

Our Vision

Friends for Matibi envisions a thriving, inclusive, and sustainable community in Matibi where all individuals have access to essential resources, opportunities, and a supportive network. We are dedicated to fostering unity, empowerment, and holistic development, creating a brighter future for Matibi and its people.

Our Holistic Support for Sustainable Progress in Matibi's Community

We believe that sustainable progress is dependent on multiple factors like education, water, regeneration, nature, health. The complexity of community development needs a multidisciplinary approach. Therefore, we are trying to balance several pillars. Education: We are operating a self-learning center called Nook. It is a school without teachers, where the passion of imagination of the learners is at the very heart of learning. We provide scholarships, school supplies, and educational resources to empower children in Matibi to reach their full potential. Alternative education is the foundation for a brighter future. Health: Our healthcare initiatives include supporting the Matibi Mission hospital, revival of surgery at Matibi, health awareness campaigns, and support for local clinics. We believe that good health is essential for individuals and the community to thrive. Food: We work towards ensuring food security through sustainable agriculture practices, community gardens, and nutrition programs. Nourishing the community ensures their well-being and vitality. Income: We empower the community by supporting income-generating activities, vocational training, and entrepreneurship. Economic stability enables families to break the cycle of poverty. Environment: The permaculture activities are meant to regenerate the soil and introduce new ways of water management. This is necessary because due to climate change the rainy season is much shorter than before. We also invest in green power by putting solar power installation on the community's roof in exchange on cattle which is better fed through the permaculture activities and then are sold in the locally-led butchery we will open to circle the money back to the community. Humans are nature, if nature is ill, we are ill too.
